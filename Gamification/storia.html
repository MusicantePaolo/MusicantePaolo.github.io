Gamification

The History of Gamification

Gamification is a concept that has deep roots in human psychology and the use of game elements to motivate and engage people. Its history is a journey that has traversed various sectors, from corporate training to marketing, education, and beyond.

1970s and 1980s

The early electronic games: The era of video games begins with the advent of early personal computers and gaming consoles. Games like Pong, Space Invaders, and Pac-Man kickstart a culture of digital gaming and entertainment.

1990s

Video games and pop culture: As video games gain popularity, pop culture begins to be influenced by iconic characters and engaging storylines. Video games become a mainstream form of entertainment.

Early 2000s

The rise of web 2.0: With the development of web 2.0, greater online interaction begins to emerge. Platforms start integrating game elements like badges, points, and leaderboards to engage users and encourage certain behaviors.

Metaverses and virtual worlds

The emergence of virtual worlds and metaverses helps expand the gaming experience. Platforms like Second Life introduce social and content creation elements, paving the way for new forms of online engagement.

Late 2000s

Birth of the term "Gamification": The term "gamification" begins to appear in the business vocabulary. Companies start exploring the use of game mechanics to increase customer and employee interest and interaction.

2010

Gamification in the corporate world: The use of gamification spreads to sectors like corporate training, marketing, and personnel management. Companies begin using game elements like badges, points, and levels to enhance engagement and achieve specific goals.

2010 - 2015

Growth and development: Gamification continues to grow in popularity. Online platforms, apps, and loyalty programs increasingly implement game elements to incentivize desired behaviors.

2016 - present

Gamification beyond digital borders: Gamification expands beyond the digital world. Sectors like health, education, and sustainability begin integrating game elements into their strategy to motivate and engage people.
